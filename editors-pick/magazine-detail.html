Better Layering For Summer: Sunscreen Routine

BridgeOn Editorial

Jul 6, 2026 Editor Nara
How editors keep a light, comfortable skincare routine while protecting glow on humid days.
img

Summer skin routines work best when each layer has a clear job. Instead of stacking heavy textures, our editors look for fresh hydration, a flexible sunscreen finish, and small reapply-friendly products that sit comfortably through the day.

Keep The First Layers Thin

Start with a watery toner or mist, then move into a serum that absorbs quickly. This keeps the base soft without making sunscreen feel slippery. If your skin gets oily by afternoon, skip rich cream in the morning and keep that step for nighttime.

The goal is not to use fewer products for the sake of it. The goal is to make every layer earn its place, so makeup and sunscreen can sit smoothly without pilling.

A Simple Editor Routine

  1. Hydrate with a toner pad or mist and press the product in lightly.
  2. Use one targeted serum for calming, brightening, or barrier support.
  3. Choose sunscreen with a finish you actually want to wear every day.
  4. Carry a compact product for midday touch-ups around cheeks and nose.
The best summer routine is the one that still feels clean after lunch.

What Editors Are Saving

Cushion sunscreen, gel creams, and airy serums are the most saved categories this week. We also like products that make reapplication feel less like a chore, especially when commuting or traveling.
